Introduction

The high speed digitizer consists of a ZCU111 Xilinx RF-SOC evaluation board with a custom analog front end (AFE) card and a front panel LCD display.

Specifications

  • Channels: 8
  • Bandwidth: DC–4 GHz
  • Sampling Rate: ~4GS/s (40 times timing system event rate)
  • Resolution: 12 bits
  • Range: Programmable from ±125mV (-8dBm) to ±2V (+16dBm) full scale in 1dB steps. Higher amplitudes with external attenuator.
  • Input Impedance: 50Ω fixed
  • Input Coupling: Programmable DC/AC
  • Record Length: ~64k points per channel
  • Calibration:  MEMS RF multiplexer can connect amplifier input to a low noise programmable voltage reference for DC calibration, or to a pseudo-random binary sequence (PRBS) training signal for ADC initialization.

Description

The signal path for each channel is as follows.

  • A pair of Schottky diodes provide protection against overvoltage input signals. The diodes are connected to the +4.25 V and -3.5 V supplies, respectively.
  • The input signal is passed though a 15 dB attenuator to limit its range to that acceptable to the following components.
  • The output of the attenuator is split with one path connected directly to the RF multiplexer and one connected to the multiplexer through a 100 nF capacitor.  These paths provide DC and AC coupling, respectively.
  • The RF multiplexer has four inputs.  The first two are as described above.  The third is connected to a calibration DAC an the fourth to a PRBS training signal.  A single DAC and training signal are connected to all eight multiplexers.  The DAC and training signal source can drive only a single 50Ω load so at most one multiplexer at a time should be set to the DAC or training signal.
  • The output of the RF multiplexer is passed through a 16 dB attenuator.
  • The output of the attenuator is connected to the + input of a differential-in, differential-out, amplifier.  Until this point the signal is single-ended with 50 Ω impedance.  From here on the signal is differential, 100 Ω.
  • The output of the first amplifier is taken through a pair of impedance matching resistors to a second differential-in, differential-out, amplifier.  This one has programmable gain over a -6 to +26 dB range, set by an I2C connection to the FPGA.
  • The output of the second amplifier is taken through an optional 1.7 GHz first-order low-pass filter to the ADC inputs.  By default the capacitor for this filter is not installed.


The differential signal processing components require power supplies at several different levels to keep the common mode at correct values.  The first amplifier runs from ±2.5 V supplies and the second from +3.75 V, -1.25 V supplies.  One supply of each level drives amplifiers for all the stages.  A ferrite bead and extensive decoupling limits the crosstalk through the power supply rails.

All power supplies are derived from the single 3.3 V supply provided by the FPGA card.  The 3.3V supply drives four power supply chains:

  • A switching step-up regulator provides 5.5 V that is then passed through another switching regulator to produce a 4.25 V supply to which the positive protection diode of each input is connected.  The 4.25 V supply also drives a linear regulator which produces the 3.75 V supply for the second stage amplifiers. 
  • A linear regulator produces the 2.5 V supply for the first stage amplifiers. 
  • An inverting switching regulator provides a -3.5 V supply to which the negative protection diode of each input is connected.  The -3.5 V supply in turn drives a linear regulator which produces the -2.5 V supply for the first stage amplifiers.
  • An inverting switching regulator provides a -2.25 V supply which in turn drives a linear regulator which produces the -1.25 V supply for the second stage amplifiers.

Other components on the board include:

  • A pair of I2C port expanders to drive the RF multiplexer switch selection lines.  A port on one of the expanders is used to read the serial number set by a DIP breakable shunt.
  • An IPMI-style EEPROM (256 bytes).
  • An I2C DAC providing a calibration voltage to each channel.
  • An FPGA output line driving an AC-coupled, low-pass filtered (~160 MHz), PRBS training signal provided to each channel.  The RF-ADCs require this signal for internal synchronization and level adjustment.  When the high speed digitizer is operating as a bunch current monitor the PRBS signal is replaced with an orbit clock.
